About Me:



Lupita Nyong'o is a Kenyan-Mexican actress and producer, born on March 1, 1983, in Mexico City. She has emerged as a prominent figure in the entertainment industry, celebrated for her exceptional talent, grace, and commitment to promoting diversity and representation in Hollywood. Beyond her successful acting career, Lupita actively engages in philanthropy and social causes. Her ongoing contributions to the film industry have earned her acclaim, making her an inspiring figure for aspiring actors worldwide.

Work and Education:

Education



Hampshire College

Lupita Nyong'o completed her undergraduate studies at Hampshire College in Amherst, Massachusetts. She earned a bachelor's degree in film and theater studies.


Yale School of Drama

After completing her undergraduate studies, Lupita went on to attend the Yale School of Drama, where she pursued a Master's degree in acting. Her time at Yale School of Drama was significant in shaping her acting skills and contributing to her later success in the film industry.

Work and Career



Lupita Nyong'o's career in the film industry is marked by exceptional talent and impactful performances. She gained international acclaim for her role as Patsey in the critically acclaimed film "12 Years a Slave," winning the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress. Lupita continued to shine with roles in blockbuster films such as "Black Panther" (2018), where she portrayed Nakia, a character celebrated for its strength and depth. Her performances have not only earned her accolades but have also made her a symbol of representation and diversity in Hollywood. Beyond her successful acting career, Lupita actively engages in philanthropy and social causes. Her commitment to promoting diversity and her ongoing contributions to the film industry have solidified her status as an inspiring figure for aspiring actors worldwide.
